2026 Sewer Rehabilitation Project

Southgate Sanitation District 2026 Sewer Rehabilitation: Sealed Bids for construction of the 2026 Sewer Rehabilitation will be received by the SOUTHGATE SANITATION DISTRICT (the "Owner") at the office of the District, 3722 East Orchard Road, Centennial, Colorado 80121, and publicly opened and read aloud at 1:00 pm on June 30, 2026. The 2026 Sewer Rehabilitation work generally consists of the following: CIPP (cured-in-place pipe) lining of approximately 1,164-linear feet of 12-inch sewer using UV-cured resin material. CIPP (cured-in-place pipe) lining of approximately 6,244-linear feet of 8-inch sewer using UV-cured resin material OR steam cured material. Both options will be considered for 8-inch CIPP, and contractors are encouraged to provide pricing for each option. Existing sewer pipe materials include vitrified clay pipe (VCP) and concrete pipe. Manholes may be traditional or drop manholes, in various conditions. Rehabilitating thirteen (13), 5-foot diameter, sanitary sewer manholes, where existing stairs are to be removed, interior walls cleaned and latent material removed, inverts raised and reshaped, walls built back with structural cementitious material, active infiltration stopped, and 137-vertical feet of topcoat lining installed. Two (2) manhole rims are to be raised 6-inches above grade and one (1) manhole rim is to be replaced. These are located in unimproved grassed areas. Work will require continuous sewer bypassing, to be completed by the Contractor. The Work will take place throughout the District&rsquo;s service area, within the City of Centennial and Greenwood Village. All permitting, traffic control, cleaning, protection of the sewers, excavating, asphalt and concrete removal and replacement, CCTV, CIPP lining, sanitary service reactivation, bypass pumping, infiltration stopping, bench build back, cementitious build back, top coating, material and coating testing, surface restoration, site restoration, and abandonments, shall be performed by the Contractor as part of this Work. The Work is expected to be performed in live flow with continuous bypass pumping required. All sanitary sewer flows shall be verified by the Contractor prior to installing and operating the temporary bypass pumping at each location.
